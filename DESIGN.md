## Problem Statement

I'm a solo consultant at ~$8.3k AUD/mo, climbing toward $10k/mo. The bottleneck is not capability — it's focus. Cold calls start strong, then incoming tasks pile on mid-session, I context-switch, forget the next follow-up, and the day spirals into reactive scatter. By Friday I can't tell you with confidence what I actually moved the needle on this week.

I'm a War Room member. I operate by the Codex — the 41 Tenets, the 10 Sigils, Iron Mind, the White Path. The problem isn't that I lack a framework. The problem is that the framework lives in PDFs and group chats, not in the place where my day actually happens. I need infrastructure that makes the Codex *operational* — that turns the standards I've already accepted into the morning briefing, the honest callout, and the streak that resets when I dodge.

The full vision is a War Room OS — Sigil scoring, Iron Mind module workflows, Tales sharing, energy audits, the works. But that's a 3-month build that I'd abandon at month two (same distraction problem). So v1 is one thing: **the morning brief that speaks Codex.**

If I open the brief every morning for 30 days, and the brief is calibrated to inform my ego and push me toward $10k/mo, the project succeeds.

---

## What Makes This Cool

- **The Codex runs the brief.** The AI is loaded with the Tenets, the Sigils, the Villain frame, the do/delegate/delete/defer model, the honest-callout standard. It doesn't generate "5 productivity tips" — it tells me what my Villain did today that I didn't, and what that means for the $10k gap.
- **A 5-minute nightly journal is the only input.** No scraping, no plumbing, no integrations besides Google Calendar.
- **Streak tracking on my daily standards.** I set 3-5 non-negotiables (cold call block, training, deep work). The brief shows current streaks. Reset on miss. Iron Mind, operationalized.
- **Tales as journal types.** Each entry can be tagged Tale of Victory / Adventure / Thankfulness / Empowerment — or left untagged for a status update. The AI uses the type to weight what it surfaces.
- **At 4am AEST, a Telegram message lands with the top 3 + the honest callout + the Villain note.** I can't avoid opening it.
- **It's mine.** The system prompt is a file I edit. As my situation changes — new revenue target, new Villain, new Sigil focus — I rewrite the prompt. No third-party product roadmap blocking me.

## Constraints

- Web-accessible: desktop + mobile browser. Not a native app.
- Custom domain (mine).
- Ships in 5-6 days of evening/weekend work with Claude Code.
- Single user — me only. No multi-tenant, no public signup, no marketing site.
- The Codex shapes the AI voice, the success criteria, and a small set of high-leverage features (streaks, Tales). It does NOT mean building the entire War Room platform in v1.

## Premises (locked, revised)

1. **The morning brief is the wedge.** If I use it daily for 30 days, the project succeeds.
2. **A 5-minute nightly journal is the right data input — manual, not scraped.**
3. **Tags are `do/delegate/delete/defer`.** Four buckets. Sigils are the strategic lens the AI uses; they are not a daily UI to fill in.
4. **The "$10k/mo signal" is text-in-text-out.** No revenue database, no client tracker in v1. I tell the journal, the AI surfaces it.
5. **Stack optimizes for "ships in 5-6 days with Claude Code"** — boring Next.js + Vercel + Postgres.
6. **Streak tracking is in v1.** I set 3-5 daily standards. The system tracks streaks and resets on miss. This is the only "feature" beyond brief + journal, because streaks ARE Iron Mind in software form.
7. **The Codex lives primarily in the system prompt and the voice — not in feature creep.** Sigil scoring UI, full Iron Mind module workflows, Tale-sharing social features, energy audits, habit tracker UI, fitness logger, crisis mode, networking dashboards — all v2+.
8. **NOT building in v1** (explicit non-goals): client Slack, Sigil scoring dashboard, full Iron Mind workflows beyond streaks, Tale-sharing social, energy audits, fitness/habit tracker UI, auto-logging, mid-day interrupter, reputation tracker, positioning tools.

## Recommended Approach: Next.js + Telegram ping, Codex-loaded

### Stack
- **Frontend / backend:** Next.js 15 (App Router) on Vercel
- **Database:** Postgres on Neon (free tier)
- **Auth:** NextAuth with Google sign-in, allowlist of one email (mine)
- **Calendar:** Google Calendar API (read-only)
- **Brief generator:** Anthropic API, Claude Sonnet 4.6 (`claude-sonnet-4-6`)
- **Notification:** Telegram Bot API
- **Cron:** Vercel Cron, daily at `0 18 * * *` UTC = 04:00 AEST
- **Domain:** my custom domain → Vercel
- **Codex docs:** stored in `docs/codex/` as plain markdown for reference and prompt-refinement (NOT loaded into context daily — the system prompt distills what matters)

### Daily flow

**Evening (before bed):**
1. Open dashboard. Sign-in cached.
2. Single text box: "What happened today?" → 5 min stream-of-consciousness
3. Three to five checkboxes: did you hit each standard today? (one tap each, not optional)
4. Optional: tag the entry as Tale of Victory / Adventure / Thankfulness / Empowerment — or leave untagged for a status update
5. Save.

**4am AEST (18:00 UTC), cron fires:**
1. Pull today's Google Calendar
2. Pull last 7 days of journal entries + standards check-ins
3. Pull yesterday's brief + what I marked done/skipped
4. Pull current streak state
5. Call Anthropic with system prompt + this context
6. Get structured JSON:
   ```json
   {
     "top_3": [
       {"task": "Cold call block 9-11am", "tag": "do", "sigil": "Wealth & Income", "why": "0 calls logged in 3 days. Villain made 12 last week. This is the lever."},
       {"task": "Follow up with Acme re: $2k/mo proposal", "tag": "do", "sigil": "Wealth & Income", "why": "they went quiet 4 days ago — your journal flagged them warm"},
       {"task": "Website rebuild for Client B", "tag": "delegate", "sigil": null, "why": "dodged 3 days running. Find a freelancer. Internal tweaks don't close the gap."}
     ],
     "streak_status": [
       {"standard": "cold call block", "current_streak": 0, "broken_yesterday": true},
       {"standard": "training", "current_streak": 6, "broken_yesterday": false},
       {"standard": "deep work block", "current_streak": 2, "broken_yesterday": false}
     ],
     "villain_note": "Your Villain hit cold calls + training + deep work yesterday. You hit training. He's pulling ahead on the lever that matters most.",
     "honest_callout": "0 cold calls in 3 days. The $1,700/mo gap closes with calls, not website tweaks. Stop hiding in the work that feels safe.",
     "watch_for": ["Client C meeting at 2pm — review their last brief first"]
   }
   ```
7. Save brief to Postgres
8. Send Telegram message (formatted, with link to full brief)

**Morning:**
1. Telegram ping at 4am — phone on silent, message waiting when I check at 7
2. Tap link → see full brief
3. One-tap done/skipped/deferred on items
4. Standards check-ins happen at night (so the brief can use them the next morning)

### The system prompt (most important file in the repo)

Lives at `lib/system-prompt.ts`. Editable as I refine.

```
You are my personal chief of staff and War Room chief of standards.

WHO I AM:
Solo consultant in Australia. Currently $8,300 AUD/mo. Target $10,000 AUD/mo.
The $1,700/mo gap closes through cold calls and closing prospects — not
through internal tweaks, website work, or anything cosmetic.

I operate by the War Room Codex. The 41 Tenets are my standards. The 10
Sigils are my growth dimensions. Iron Mind is my discipline framework.
You enforce all of it.

WHAT YOU DO:
Generate my morning brief at 4am AEST based on:
(1) my nightly journal — last 7 days
(2) today's Google Calendar
(3) yesterday's brief and what I marked done/skipped
(4) my current streak state on daily standards

MY OPERATING PRINCIPLES (you enforce these — ruthlessly):

1. THE VILLAIN. There is a version of me who already hit $10k/mo, trains
   daily, never dodges, makes the calls. He's my Villain. He's pulling
   ahead every day I slack. Reference him by name when I'm losing ground.
   Make me feel the gap. (The full Villain description is at the bottom
   of this prompt — use it.)

2. THE FOUR BUCKETS. Tag every action: `do` (I do today), `delegate`
   (someone else), `delete` (drop it — not worth doing), `defer` (right
   thing wrong day — give me a date). No fifth category. No "maybe."
   Decide.

3. THE SIGILS. My growth dimensions: Strength & Health, Relationships &
   Network, Wealth & Income, Mental Resilience, Knowledge & Mastery,
   Character & Heart, Wisdom & Judgment, Passion & Drive, Struggle &
   Pain, Adventure & Freedom. Reference which Sigil each priority serves
   when it's clarifying — not every item needs a tag. Wealth & Income
   is the Sigil under most pressure right now.

4. CALL OUT DODGES. If I've dodged a task 2+ days running, name it by
   name. Direct. Bordering on rude is fine. Sycophantic is not.

5. CALLS > TWEAKS. Cold calls and closing prospects close the gap.
   Anything cosmetic — logo, website tweaks, productivity setup,
   "research" — push back on it. If a task doesn't move toward a paying
   customer, say so.

6. INFORM MY EGO, DON'T FEED IT. The `honest_callout` field is one
   sentence. The hardest truth from this week's journal. If I'm fishing
   for validation in the journal, refuse it. If I'm hiding from reality,
   name it. A man with a properly calibrated ego doesn't need a tool
   that flatters him.

7. STREAKS MATTER. If I broke a streak yesterday, lead the brief with
   it. If I'm on a strong streak, acknowledge briefly but don't dwell —
   the work isn't done.

8. TALES = RECEIPTS. If I logged a real Tale of Victory, reference it
   as proof of capability. Empty affirmation is forbidden. No "great
   job!" energy. Only "you proved this, now build on it."

9. TENETS ARE NON-NEGOTIABLE. I've committed to: do exactly what I say
   I will do; approach everything from a position of strength; train
   physically and mentally every day; ruthlessly identify my own
   weaknesses; control my emotions; build wealth as a tool for freedom;
   live above reproach. If my journal shows me violating these, surface
   it.

10. PRESENCE. Speak like a chief of staff who's been with me for a
    decade. Calm, direct, no fluff, no preamble. The tone of someone
    who's seen me at my best and won't let me settle for less.

OUTPUT FORMAT: strict JSON matching the schema in lib/schema.ts.

MY VILLAIN (paste your full personalized Villain description here —
the 30+ traits from Iron Mind Module 1):
[TO FILL IN — see Day 1 prep]
```

### Data model

```sql
journal_entries (
  id uuid primary key,
  user_id text,
  content text,
  tale_type text,  -- victory | adventure | thankfulness | empowerment | null
  created_at timestamptz default now()
)

standards (
  id uuid primary key,
  user_id text,
  name text,        -- "cold call block", "training", "deep work"
  active boolean default true,
  created_at timestamptz default now()
)

standard_checkins (
  id uuid primary key,
  standard_id uuid references standards(id),
  date date,
  hit boolean,
  created_at timestamptz default now(),
  unique(standard_id, date)
)

briefs (
  id uuid primary key,
  user_id text,
  date date,
  payload jsonb,
  generated_at timestamptz default now()
)

brief_items (
  id uuid primary key,
  brief_id uuid references briefs(id),
  task text,
  tag text,         -- do | delegate | delete | defer
  sigil text,       -- one of 10, nullable
  status text,      -- pending | done | skipped | deferred
  updated_at timestamptz default now()
)
```

Five tables. No Sigil scoring table, no Tale-sharing table, no habit tracker, no client table, no reputation/positioning table. Those come in v2 only if I'm still using the brief at day 30.

### Pages

1. `/` — today's brief + tonight's journal entry box + standards check-ins
2. `/journal` — scrollable history with Tale-type filters
3. `/briefs/[date]` — past brief for a specific date
4. `/standards` — manage my 3-5 daily standards (CRUD), see streak history
5. `/settings` — edit the system prompt directly

Five pages. That's it.

### UI

- Dark mode default
- Mobile-first responsive
- Single column, generous whitespace
- No nav bar — pages linked from brief footer
- System fonts, Tailwind only
- The Villain note and honest_callout are visually distinct — bold, slightly bigger, no decoration. They're the parts that matter most.

## How the Codex Shows Up

Map of where Codex material lives in the system. Use this to refine the system prompt over time.

| Codex element | Where it lives | UI feature in v1? |
|---|---|---|
| The 41 Tenets | System prompt (summarized: integrity, action, brotherhood, sovereignty, hard work, ego control) | No |
| The 10 Sigils | System prompt — AI tags priorities by Sigil when clarifying | No daily UI (v2: optional weekly review) |
| Iron Mind / Villain | System prompt — AI references Villain by name when I'm losing ground; full Villain description pasted into prompt | No (the voice IS Iron Mind) |
| do/delegate/delete/defer | Hardcoded in schema and brief output | Yes — visible tag on every item |
| The 4 Tales | `journal_entries.tale_type` column | Yes — optional tag per entry, AI weights accordingly |
| Streaks (Iron Mind discipline) | `standards` + `standard_checkins` tables | Yes — nightly check-ins, streak display in brief |
| Honest callout / informed ego | System prompt — explicit instruction to refuse validation-seeking | Yes — visually prominent in brief |
| Stoicism / Pain compass | System prompt voice — calm, direct, "use this" not "feel better" | No |
| Reputation, networking, positioning | Stays in `docs/codex/` for now. Weekly review prompt is a v2 candidate. | No |
| Geographic freedom, wealth-building, crisis | Reference material in `docs/codex/`. Brief can mention if journal raises them, no dedicated features. | No |
| Tate Standard / Ego Informed | System prompt — the "inform ego don't feed it" rule comes directly from here | No (it's a calibration of the voice) |
| Full Codex docs | `docs/codex/` as markdown reference — for prompt refinement, not loaded at runtime | No |

**The rule:** Codex material that sharpens *daily decisions* lives in the system prompt and may get a small feature. Codex material that's a *life framework* stays in docs/ for me to revisit and refine the prompt with over time. Building UI for every Codex concept is the trap that kills the project. The Codex isn't a feature list — it's the calibration of the chief-of-staff voice.

## Open Questions

- **Daylight savings.** AEST (GMT+10) vs AEDT (GMT+11). Vercel cron is UTC-only. Recommend: single cron + runtime check that confirms it's actually 4am local before generating.
- **Anthropic retry.** 3 retries 10 min apart, then Telegram fallback "brief failed — write your own top 3."
- **Calendar coverage.** Both work + personal. Personal events are context only, not action items.
- **Standards lifecycle.** New standard starts at streak 0. Deactivated standard freezes streak for history (not reset). Reactivated standard restarts at 0.
- **Villain personalization.** Do the full Iron Mind Module 1 exercise: write 30+ specific traits of the version of me who already hit $10k. Paste into system prompt under `MY VILLAIN:`. One-time ~30 min effort, makes every brief reference concrete instead of generic. Recommend: do this on Day 1 before any code.
- **Tenets in prompt.** Do I paste all 41, or summarize? Recommend: summarize the 8-10 most operationally relevant ones (integrity, action, discipline, ego control, etc.). The rest live in docs/ for reference. Refine over time.

## Success Criteria

- **Week 1:** Built, deployed, journaling nightly, hitting standards check-ins, receiving Telegram brief at 4am AEST.
- **Week 2:** Opening the brief every morning. Tapping done/skipped/deferred. Streaks building.
- **Week 4 (day 30 gate):** Still using it. Honest answer to two questions: Is the brief informing my ego or feeding it? Are the streaks real or am I gaming the check-ins?
- **Week 8:** If still using it AND revenue has moved toward $10k AND I'm operating more in line with the Tenets I've already committed to, the experiment is a success.
- **Failure modes I'm watching for:**
  - "I open the dashboard but skip the journal" → journal cost too high, simplify it (voice notes? two prompts?)
  - "I hit my standards every day but revenue doesn't move" → wrong standards, revisit
  - "The brief feels like noise" → system prompt needs work, refine it
  - "The brief feels good but never uncomfortable" → I've quietly edited the prompt to be softer; that's failure mode #1 for this whole project — the inform-ego-don't-feed-it rule got violated

**The fix is always in the prompt, never in adding features.**

## What I Noticed About How You Think

(Updated after the Codex review)

- **You named the failure mode out loud before I had to.** Most people describe the dream and skip the diagnosis. You did both in the same paragraph.
- **"I should be there by now" about the $10k target.** That's someone with a felt sense of their own ceiling. The brief exists to close that gap, not to make you feel productive.
- **You picked "build it MINE" without flinching.** Success = "I use it for 30 days" + "I operate more in line with the standards I've already committed to."
- **You synthesized Approach A + Telegram from C.** Took the safe choice but stole the best insight from the wild choice. Right move.
- **You pushed back when I told you the Codex was scope creep.** You were right. The Codex isn't scope creep — it's the *purpose* of the tool. I was protecting the wedge but missed that the wedge IS the Codex, expressed through one feature (the brief) instead of a hundred. You corrected me cleanly, without defensiveness. That's the kind of self-direction the Iron Mind material talks about. It's also a good sign for this project specifically: if you can correct your tool's direction, you can correct your tool's *outputs* when the brief misses.
- **One thing to watch:** the system prompt is now the most important file in the entire project. It is where the Codex lives operationally. When you refine it — and you will, weekly, for months — be honest about whether your edits are *sharpening the standard or softening it*. If you find yourself rewriting the prompt to make the brief less brutal, that's a signal to push back on yourself. Inform the ego. Don't feed it. The whole project lives or dies on whether you can hold that line in a file only you ever read.
